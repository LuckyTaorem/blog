### The Role of the FCC
The FCC is the primary authority for regulating the use of the airwaves in the United States. This includes issuing licenses for broadcasters, such as television and radio stations, as well as for satellite and cellular communications. The FCC also plays a crucial role in protecting consumers from unwanted robocalls and ensuring that public safety agencies have the necessary resources to perform their duties.

Some of the key responsibilities of the FCC include:
* Issuing licenses for the use of the airwaves
* Regulating broadcast television and radio
* Protecting consumers from unwanted robocalls
* Ensuring public safety agencies have necessary resources
* Negotiating internationally for the use of the airwaves

* Developing and implementing regulations for the telecommunications industry
* Overseeing the enforcement of FCC regulations
* Representing the FCC in international negotiations and forums
