## Introduction to the LG Micro RGB Evo
The LG Micro RGB Evo is a high-end TV that utilizes micro RGB technology to provide improved color accuracy, contrast, and brightness. This technology is designed to offer a more immersive viewing experience, making it ideal for gaming, watching movies, and streaming content. In this article, we will delve into the features, specifications, and pricing of the LG Micro RGB Evo, as well as its competitors in the market.

### Key Features of the LG Micro RGB Evo
The LG Micro RGB Evo boasts an impressive array of features that set it apart from other TVs in its class. Some of the key features include:
* 100% color gamut specs for BT.2020, DCI-P3, and Adobe RGB
* 4 HDMI 2.1 ports, including one for e ARC passthrough audio
* 1 Ethernet port, 1 digital optical, 1 coaxial connection, and 2 USB 2.0 ports
* Wi-Fi 5 connectivity
* Web OS 26 operating system
* Supports 165-Hz refresh rate and low latency for gaming
* Variable Refresh Rate (VRR) mode with 330 Hz support (not working properly)

### Pricing and Availability
The LG Micro RGB Evo is available in 75-inch, 86-inch, and 100-inch sizes, with prices ranging from $5,000 to $8,000. This puts it in the premium category, competing with other high-end TVs from brands like Samsung, Hisense, and Sony. For example, the Samsung Micro RGB R95H is priced at $3,000 for a 65-inch model, while the TCL RGB Mini-LED RM9L costs $6,000 for an 85-inch model.

### Comparison with Other TVs
In terms of pricing, the LG Micro RGB Evo is one of the most expensive options in the market. However, its features and specifications make it a strong contender in the high-end TV segment. For instance, the Hisense UR9 RGB Mini LED is priced at $2,200 for a 65-inch model, while the Sony Bravia 7 Mark II costs $2,600 for a 65-inch model.
