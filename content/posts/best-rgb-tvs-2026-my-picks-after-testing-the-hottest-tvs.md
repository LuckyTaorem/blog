## Introduction to Mini RGB Technology
The television industry is undergoing a significant transformation with the introduction of mini RGB technology. This innovative technology emits red, green, and blue lights through an LCD panel, resulting in better colors, improved contrast, and higher brightness. Major manufacturers such as Hisense, Samsung, LG, Sony, and TCL are already incorporating mini RGB technology into their products.

## Key Features and Products
Some notable products that utilize mini RGB technology include:
* LG Micro RGB Evo: This TV features four HDMI 2.1 ports, an Ethernet port, and two USB 2.0 ports. It also has a streaming platform called Web OS and offers various picture modes, including Filmmaker mode and Vivid mode. The LG Micro RGB Evo is priced at $4,500 for the 75-inch model.
* Hisense UR9 RGB Mini LED: This TV has three HDMI 2.1 ports, a Display Port USB-C connection, and an Ethernet port. It runs on Google TV and is known for its outstanding picture quality, especially for video games. The Hisense UR9 RGB Mini LED is priced at $2,200 for the 65-inch model.
* TCL RM9L RGB-Mini LED: This TV features four HDMI 2.1 ports, a 144-Hz refresh rate for gaming, and ports for USB devices, coaxial, and an Ethernet connection. It also runs on Google TV and offers vivid colors, although its massive size can reveal some picture quality issues. The TCL RM9L RGB-Mini LED is available in sizes 85-inch and larger, with prices starting at $9,000 for the 98-inch model.

## Pricing and Availability
The prices of mini RGB TVs are expected to decrease soon, making them more accessible to consumers. Currently, OLED models from LG and Samsung cost around $2,700 for a 65-inch model, while LED and QLED models can be found for as low as $500. The Hisense UR9 RGB Mini LED is the cheapest mini RGB model available, priced at $2,200 for the 65-inch model.
