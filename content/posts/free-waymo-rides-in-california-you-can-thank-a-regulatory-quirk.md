## Introduction to Waymo's Robotaxi Service
Waymo, a subsidiary of Alphabet, has been at the forefront of autonomous vehicle technology. Their robotaxi service has been expanding to various parts of the United States, with a focus on providing safe and efficient transportation to the public. Recently, Waymo has been facing regulatory issues in California, which have led to a unique situation where passengers can ride in their new vehicle, the Ojai, for free.

### Background on the Regulatory Issues
The California Public Utilities Commission (CPUC) has been reviewing Waymo's application to expand their service in Northern and Southern California. However, due to a delayed decision, Waymo is not allowed to charge passengers for rides in the Ojai vehicle. This has resulted in a temporary situation where passengers can ride in the Ojai for free, at least until the end of September. As [reported earlier](/posts/robotaxi-recall), Waymo has faced similar issues in the past, including a recall of their robotaxis due to a risk of driving into freeway construction zones.

## Technical Specifications of the Ojai Vehicle
The Ojai vehicle is a significant improvement over previous models, with a range of advanced features and sensors. Some of the key technical specifications of the Ojai include:
* 13 cameras for 360-degree vision
* 6 radar systems for detecting obstacles
* 4 lidar sensors for mapping the environment
* Connected systems built and added in the US, with the vehicle manufactured by Zeekr, a Chinese company

These advanced sensors and systems enable the Ojai to navigate complex environments and provide a safe and comfortable ride for passengers.
