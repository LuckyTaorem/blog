## Introduction to Data Center Construction Ban
New York has become the first state in the US to enact a ban on data center construction, aiming to promote environmental sustainability. This move is expected to have significant implications for the tech industry, particularly for companies reliant on large data centers. Governor Kathy Hochul signed an executive order pausing environmental permits for large data centers consuming more than 50 megawatts for up to a year. As reported, "New York will lead the way in creating the strongest standards in the nation for data center development, ensuring that when companies succeed because of New York, New Yorkers succeed too."

## Background and Motivation
The decision to ban data center construction is motivated by concerns over the environmental impact of these facilities. Data centers require massive amounts of energy to operate, which can lead to increased utility bills, depletion of natural resources, and uncertainty for local communities. Governor Hochul emphasized the need for action, stating, "As data center development threatens to hike up utility bills, deplete our natural resources and create uncertainty for New Yorkers, it's my responsibility to take action and lead." This move is part of a broader effort to promote sustainable development and reduce the state's carbon footprint. ## Key Aspects of the Executive Order
The executive order will take effect immediately and will not affect projects that already have the required permits. It is separate from New York's Responsible Data Center Development Act, which was recently passed by the state legislature. The Responsible Data Center Development Act would have banned data center permits for a year, but required energy efficiency goals for data centers and specific benefits for host communities. Governor Hochul has yet to sign the Responsible Data Center Development Act but is considering it.
