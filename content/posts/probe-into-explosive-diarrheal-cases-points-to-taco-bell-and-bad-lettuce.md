## Introduction to the Outbreak
A diarrheal parasite outbreak, specifically Cyclospora, is currently surging nationwide, with an extraordinary number of cases reported in Michigan. According to the Michigan Department of Health and Human Services (MDHHS), the outbreak is linked to lettuce and salad greens. This situation highlights the importance of food safety and the need for proper handling and inspection of produce to prevent such outbreaks.

## The Scope of the Outbreak
As of July 14, Michigan has reported 3,309 cases of cyclosporiasis, which is significantly higher than the typical annual count of around 50 cases. The outbreak has also resulted in 44 hospitalizations, emphasizing the severity of the situation. Interviews with over 1,000 people sickened in Michigan point to leafy greens as the source of the outbreak, reinforcing the connection between the contaminated produce and the surge in Cyclospora cases.

### Understanding Cyclospora
Cyclospora is a parasite that can cause cyclosporiasis, an intestinal infection. The symptoms of cyclosporiasis can include diarrhea, weight loss, and stomach cramps, among others. It is typically spread through the consumption of contaminated food or water. Given the link between the current outbreak and lettuce/salad greens, it is crucial for consumers to be aware of the sources of their produce and for farmers/distributors to ensure that their products are safely handled and inspected.

## Preventive Measures and Awareness
Preventing the spread of Cyclospora involves a combination of proper food handling, thorough washing of produce, and awareness of potential contamination sources. Consumers can reduce their risk by washing lettuce and salad greens thoroughly before consumption and being cautious of the origin and handling history of their produce.
