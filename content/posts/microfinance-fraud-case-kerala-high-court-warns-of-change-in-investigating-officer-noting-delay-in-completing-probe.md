## Introduction to the Microfinance Fraud Case
The Kerala High Court has recently directed the officer investigating the SNDP microfinance case to complete the probe within three weeks. This directive comes after the court observed that the investigating officer is not properly investigating the crimes as directed by the court. The case relates to the alleged misappropriation of Rs. 15.85 crores of government funds under the Micro Finance Scheme, involving officers of the SNDP Yogam and former Managing Directors of the Kerala State Backward Classes Development Corporation Ltd. (KSBCDC).

### Background of the Case
The Micro Finance Scheme was aimed at aiding members of marginalized communities through the SNDP Yogam. However, it is alleged that the SNDP officials and KSBCDC MDs colluded to misappropriate the government loan of Rs. 15.85 crores that was allotted during 2003 to 2014. The case was registered by the Vigilance and Anti-Corruption Bureau (VACB) in 2020, and the court had granted many extensions to the investigating officers appointed.

### Investigation and Court Proceedings
The court had directed the investigating officer to complete the probe by June 20 and to file reports in all cases. However, when another extension was sought, Justice A. Badharudeen orally remarked that he would order another investigating officer, stating that the current officer might not be able to complete the investigation due to some pressure. The court has been considering pleas registered in 2020 seeking the appointment of a Special Investigation Team headed by an efficient officer to investigate the crime.

## Key Players and Their Roles
The key players in the case include:
* **SNDP Yogam**: The organization through which the Micro Finance Scheme was implemented.
* **KSBCDC**: The Kerala State Backward Classes Development Corporation Ltd., whose former Managing Directors are involved in the case.
* **VACB**: The Vigilance and Anti-Corruption Bureau, which registered the case in 2020.
* **Investigating Officer**: The officer appointed by the court to investigate the case, currently S. Sasidharan IPS, Superintendent of Police, VACB, Central Range, Ernakulam.
* **Justice A. Badharudeen**: The judge who has been hearing the case and directing the investigating officer to complete the probe.

### Previous Investigating Officers
Previous investigating officers include:
* **Dr. J. Himendranath IPS**: Who was appointed as the investigating officer as per the directions of the court but was relieved from duty when he was posted as Superintendent of Police.

## Court Orders and Directives
The court has issued several orders and directives in the case, including:
* **Direction to complete probe within three weeks**: The court has directed the investigating officer to complete the probe within three weeks and to file reports in all cases.
* **Warning of change in investigating officer**: The court has warned that if the investigating officer is not able to complete the investigation within the specified time, it would be constrained to consider entrusting the investigation to another officer.
* **Appointment of Special Investigation Team**: The court has been considering pleas seeking the appointment of a Special Investigation Team headed by an efficient officer to investigate the crime.
