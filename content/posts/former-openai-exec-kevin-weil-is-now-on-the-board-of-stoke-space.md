## Introduction to Stoke Space and Kevin Weil
The space industry has been abuzz with the latest development in reusable rocket technology, courtesy of Stoke Space, a Seattle-based startup. In a significant move, Kevin Weil, a former OpenAI executive, has joined the board of Stoke Space, bringing his expertise in AI and technology to the table. This move is expected to give a significant boost to Stoke Space's mission to develop completely reusable rockets. As [reported by Tech Crunch](/posts/with-starfall-spacex-eyes-an-edge-in-global-cargo-delivery-from-orbit/), the space industry is witnessing a surge in investments and innovations, and Stoke Space is poised to be a key player in this landscape.

## Stoke Space's Mission and Technology
Stoke Space is on a mission to develop reusable rockets that can be flown again and again, significantly reducing the cost of space travel. The company's rocket, Nova, is designed to survive the extreme heat of reentering the Earth's atmosphere from space, a technological challenge that has been a major hurdle in the development of reusable rockets. As [reported by Tim Fernholz](/posts/with-starfall-spacex-eyes-an-edge-in-global-cargo-delivery-from-orbit/), the development of reusable rockets is a key area of focus for space companies, including SpaceX, founded by [Elon Musk](/posts/with-starfall-spacex-eyes-an-edge-in-global-cargo-delivery-from-orbit/).

### Key Features of Nova
Some of the key features of Nova include:
* Completely reusable design
* Ability to survive extreme heat of reentry
* Distributed data centers in space to leverage solar power and escape political restrictions on Earth
* Potential for significant cost savings in space travel
