## Introduction to the Luddite Movement
The Summer of Ludd, a Luddite festival, took place in New York earlier this month, promoting a movement against technology's negative impact on society. This movement is not just about moving away from Big Tech tools but also about creating alternative infrastructure and technology that serves people. The festival included workshops, events, and activities to encourage people to engage with the world in a more meaningful way. As [Valve's Steam Machine](https://ltdeveloperblogs.github.io/posts/valve-is-working-with-amd-to-bring-fsr-4-to-the-steam-machine/) and other tech advancements continue to shape our world, it's essential to consider the potential downsides of technology.

## The Festival and Its Events
The Summer of Ludd festival included events such as "What Are You Doing Tonight? How to Create Your Own Events Calendar" and "Luddite Rizz" (a workshop on how to flirt IRL). These events aim to create an in-person community and bring people back into public space. By doing so, the movement is not just about technology but also about social issues, labor issues, and environmental issues. As seen in the [Uber Board Sued](https://ltdeveloperblogs.github.io/posts/ubers-board-sued-for-alleged-poor-oversight-that-led-to-sexual-abuse-of-its-passengers/) case, technology can have significant social implications.

### The Original Luddites
The original Luddites were British textile workers who organized against being replaced by technology during the Industrial Revolution. The term "Luddite" has been used as a derogatory term to refer to someone who opposes technology, but the Summer of Ludd movement is trying to reclaim the term and give it a new meaning. This movement is not against technology itself but against the negative impact it can have on society. As [Venus Aerospace's $90M RDRE](https://ltdeveloperblogs.github.io/posts/venus-aerospace-raises-90m-series-b-to-build-a-new-kind-of-rocket-engine/) investment shows, technology can be a powerful tool for progress when used responsibly.

## Quotes from the Festival
Gowanus, a media puppet representing the Summer of Ludd movement, quoted the original Luddites, saying, "We are against machines harmful to commonality that tears unduly at the social fabric." Gowanus also emphasized the importance of creating technology that serves people, stating, "We're actually really big on creating technology, which is kind of contradictory." This highlights the movement's focus on using technology to improve society, rather than simply rejecting it. As discussed in [Valve Enables DIY Steam Machines](https://ltdeveloperblogs.github.io/posts/valve-will-finally-let-you-build-your-own-steam-machine-with-steamos-for-desktop/), the ability to create and customize technology can be a powerful tool for positive change.
