## Introduction to Data Center Moratorium
New York has taken a significant step towards regulating the growth of hyperscale data centers in the state. Governor Kathy Hochul has enacted a data center moratorium, which blocks new environmental permits for hyperscale data centers over 50 megawatts. This move is expected to give the state time to come up with regulations needed to protect residents from rising energy prices and environmental impact.

## Background and Context
The moratorium is a response to the growing concern about the environmental impact of hyperscale data centers. These data centers consume large amounts of energy and water, and their construction can lead to environmental degradation. The state legislature had approved a bill with a 20 megawatt threshold, but the enacted moratorium has a higher threshold of 50 megawatts. Another bill that could restrict even more developments is awaiting Governor Hochul's signature.
