### The Role of DMCA Takedown Requests
At the heart of this issue are DMCA (Digital Millennium Copyright Act) takedown requests. The DMCA is a U.S. copyright law that implements two 1996 treaties of the World Intellectual Property Organization (WIPO). It criminalizes production and dissemination of technology, devices, or services intended to circumvent measures that control access to copyrighted works. It also heightens the penalties for copyright infringement on the Internet. When a creator believes their content is being used without permission, they can file a DMCA takedown notice to the platform hosting the infringing content, requesting its removal.

The process, while designed to protect creators' rights, can sometimes lead to false positives or the removal of content that is not infringing. This is particularly problematic when automated systems are used to scan for and report copyright infringement, as they may not always accurately distinguish between legitimate and infringing uses of content.
