## Introduction to Uber's Expansion
Uber is expanding its services beyond ride-hailing, introducing new features and services to enhance user experience. According to Sachin Kansal, Uber's Chief Product Officer, the company is focusing on laying down the foundation for multiple players to work together, rather than competing to be an L4 autonomy provider. This strategy is evident in Uber's partnerships with companies like Expedia for hotel bookings and Waymo for autonomous vehicle development.

## New Features and Services
Some of the new features and services introduced by Uber include:
* Hotel bookings powered by Expedia
* "Shop for me" concierge features
* Boat rentals in Europe
* Debit cards for drivers
* Data-labeling side hustle for drivers
* Uber Pro card, a debit card for drivers and couriers
* Uber credits, a currency for consumers to use
* Uber One membership product
* Grocery cart assistant on Uber Eats
* Voice-to-request ride feature on Uber
* Earner assistant on Uber
* AV Labs, a business unit developing a fleet of sensor-equipped vehicles
* Autonomous vehicle data collection and labeling

## Partnerships and Collaborations
Uber's partnerships with other companies are a key aspect of its expansion strategy. The company has partnered with Expedia for hotel bookings, allowing users to book hotels directly through the Uber app. Additionally, Uber has partnered with Waymo for autonomous vehicle development, which is expected to enhance the safety and efficiency of Uber's services. ## Uber One Membership Product
The Uber One membership product is a key aspect of Uber's expansion strategy. With 51 million members, the Uber One membership product is a significant contributor to Uber's revenue. Half of bookings on Uber come from Uber One members, who enjoy benefits such as Uber credits and exclusive discounts.
