## Introduction to Foldable Displays
The concept of foldable displays has been around for several years, with various manufacturers experimenting with different designs and technologies. One of the most significant challenges in developing foldable displays is ensuring their durability and reliability. Samsung Display, a leading manufacturer of display panels, has been working closely with Apple to develop OLED panels for the upcoming foldable iPhone.

### History of Foldable Displays
The first foldable display was introduced by Samsung in 2019, with the launch of the Galaxy Fold. However, the device was plagued by issues with its hinge and display, which led to a delayed release. Since then, several other manufacturers, including Huawei and Motorola, have released their own foldable devices. Despite the initial setbacks, the technology has improved significantly, and we are now seeing more reliable and durable foldable displays.

## Samsung Display's OLED Panels
Samsung Display has received Apple's approval to begin module production of OLED panels for the foldable iPhone. This is a significant milestone, as it indicates that Samsung Display has met Apple's stringent quality and reliability standards. The OLED panels will be produced at Samsung Display's facility in Vietnam, which has a total of 80 production lines. Currently, around 50 lines are active, leaving ample capacity to spare given the initial order of three million panels.

### Key Features of Samsung Display's OLED Panels
Some of the key features of Samsung Display's OLED panels include:
* Color Filter on Encapsulation (CoE) technology, which removes the polarizer and forms a color filter directly on top of the encapsulation layer
* Samsung Display's newest M16 OLED material set, which brings improvements to brightness, color performance, lifespan, and power efficiency over prior generations
* A yield rate of over 80%, which is significantly higher than Apple's threshold of 70%

### Benefits of OLED Panels
OLED panels offer several benefits over traditional LCD displays, including:
* Higher contrast ratios, resulting in deeper blacks and more vivid colors
* Faster response times, making them ideal for fast-paced content like video games and action movies
* Lower power consumption, resulting in longer battery life
* Thinner and lighter designs, making them ideal for portable devices

## Exclusive Agreement between Apple and Samsung Display
Samsung Display has signed an exclusive agreement with Apple to supply OLED panels for the foldable iPhone. This agreement is valid for three years, during which time Apple will not source foldable OLED panels from any other display maker. This is a significant win for Samsung Display, as it will provide a steady stream of revenue for the company.

### Implications of the Exclusive Agreement
The exclusive agreement between Apple and Samsung Display has several implications, including:
* Samsung Display will have a monopoly on the supply of OLED panels for the foldable iPhone, giving the company significant negotiating power
* Apple will be able to ensure a consistent supply of high-quality OLED panels, which will help to maintain the quality of the foldable iPhone
* The agreement will help to drive innovation in the development of OLED panels, as Samsung Display will have a strong incentive to continue improving its technology
