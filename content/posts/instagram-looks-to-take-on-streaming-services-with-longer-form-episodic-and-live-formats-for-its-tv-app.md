## Introduction to Instagram's New Venture
Instagram, a social media platform owned by Meta, is expanding its reach into the world of streaming services. The company has announced plans to introduce longer-form, episodic, and live formats to its TV app, which was launched last year. This move is seen as an attempt to bring Instagram to more TV users and capture a larger share of the streaming market.

### Background on Instagram's TV App
Instagram's TV app was first launched in 2022, allowing users to access Instagram content on their TVs. The app was initially available on Amazon Fire and Google TV, but it has now been rolled out to Samsung TVs as well. The TV app provides users with a unique way to experience Instagram content, including Reels, IGTV, and live streams.

### New Features and Formats
The new features and formats being introduced to Instagram's TV app include:
* Longer-form videos: Instagram will now allow creators to upload longer-form videos to the platform, providing more opportunities for in-depth content and storytelling.
* Episodic series: Instagram will introduce episodic series, allowing creators to produce and publish multi-part content.
* Live TV: Instagram will also introduce live TV, allowing creators to broadcast live to their audiences.
* Channels: The TV app will feature channels, which will be tailored to the creators and topics that users are interested in.
* Casting: Users will be able to cast Reels and other content from their phone to their TV.
* Horizontal videos: The TV app will support horizontal videos, making it easier to watch content on TV.
* Stories: Users will be able to view other users' Stories within the TV app.
