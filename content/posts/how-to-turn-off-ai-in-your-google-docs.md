## Introduction
Google Docs has become an essential tool for many of us, providing a convenient and collaborative way to create and edit documents. However, some users have been experiencing a new feature that can be more of a nuisance than a help: the "Write with Gemini" pop-ups. These AI-powered suggestions are designed to assist with writing, but they can be distracting and disrupt the creative flow. In this article, we'll explore how to turn off these AI pop-ups and restore a distraction-free writing experience in Google Docs.

## What are the "Write with Gemini" Pop-ups?
Before we dive into the solution, let's take a closer look at what these pop-ups are and what they're intended to do. The "Write with Gemini" feature is a new AI-powered tool that uses machine learning algorithms to analyze your writing and provide suggestions for improvement. These suggestions can range from grammar and spelling corrections to more substantive recommendations for rephrasing sentences or reorganizing paragraphs.

While the idea behind this feature is to help users write more effectively, the reality is that these pop-ups can be intrusive and annoying. They can appear at random intervals, interrupting your train of thought and making it difficult to focus on your writing. If you're finding these pop-ups to be more of a hindrance than a help, don't worry – there is a way to turn them off.

## How to Turn Off the "Write with Gemini" Pop-ups
Fortunately, disabling the "Write with Gemini" pop-ups is a relatively simple process. Here are the steps to follow:

* Log in to your Google Docs account and open the document you're working on.
* Click on the "Tools" menu in the top navigation bar.
* Select "Preferences" from the drop-down menu.
* In the "Preferences" dialog box, scroll down to the "Write with Gemini" section.
* Uncheck the box next to "Enable Write with Gemini."
* Click "OK" to save your changes.

By following these steps, you should be able to turn off the "Write with Gemini" pop-ups and enjoy a distraction-free writing experience in Google Docs.

* **Google Docs' Built-in Tools**: Google Docs has its own built-in tools for checking grammar, spelling, and punctuation. You can access these tools by clicking on the "Tools" menu and selecting "Spelling and grammar" or "Dictionary."
