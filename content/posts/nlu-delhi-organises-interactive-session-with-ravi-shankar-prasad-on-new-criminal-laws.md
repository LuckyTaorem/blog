## Introduction to the Interactive Session
The National Law University (NLU) Delhi, in collaboration with the Institute of Constitutional and Parliamentary Studies (ICPS), New Delhi, recently organised a credit-based certificate course on the new criminal laws. This course aimed to deepen the understanding of India's new criminal laws among participants. As part of this course, NLU Delhi hosted an interactive session with former Union Cabinet Minister and Member of Lok Sabha, Ravi Shankar Prasad, on June 18. This session provided a unique opportunity for participants to engage with a renowned expert and gain insights into the historical, constitutional, and civilisational context of India's new criminal laws.

### Significance of the Course
The course on new criminal laws is significant in today's context, as it helps to promote a better understanding of the criminal justice system and its evolution. By exploring the historical and constitutional context of the new laws, participants can gain a deeper appreciation for the complexities and challenges involved in criminal law reform. The course also underscores the importance of critical reflection and public engagement in advancing meaningful criminal justice reforms.

### Vice-Chancellor's Welcome Address
The Vice-Chancellor of NLU Delhi, Prof. (Dr.) G.S. Bajpai, welcomed Ravi Shankar Prasad and highlighted the significance of the course in deepening the understanding of India's new criminal laws. He also underscored the University's contribution to the country's criminal law reforms and acknowledged the collaboration of ICPS in advancing constitutional, parliamentary, and legal education initiatives. The Vice-Chancellor's address set the tone for the interactive session, emphasizing the importance of academic institutions in promoting critical thinking and public engagement.
