## Introduction to Meta AI Glasses
Meta, a renowned technology company, has been working on improving the reputation of its AI glasses by announcing an update that disables the camera if the LED light indicating recording has been tampered with. This move aims to address the growing concerns about privacy and surveillance. However, Meta's AI strategy seems to contradict this goal by pushing products and features that collect more user data. In this article, we will delve into the details of Meta's AI glasses, their features, and the implications of Meta's AI strategy on user privacy.

## Features of Meta AI Glasses
The Meta AI glasses come equipped with a camera that can be disabled if the LED light is tampered with. This feature is designed to provide users with an added layer of security and privacy. Additionally, the glasses have a prototype that can continuously collect audio while taking photos every few seconds. This feature raises concerns about the potential for surveillance and the collection of sensitive user data. The Meta AI app also has poor privacy controls, allowing users to reveal embarrassing searches. Furthermore, Meta AI can use anyone's public Instagram photos to make AI images, unless opted out. The company can also use images in the Camera Roll that have never been shared.

### Meta's Statement on Privacy
Meta has stated that "You, and only you — unless you choose to share them" can see photos and videos taken on Meta AI glasses. However, this statement is contradicted by the company's AI strategy, which prioritizes the collection of user data. On its Privacy Progress Update page, Meta claims to have "invested significantly in people, products, and technology to continue to evolve our rigorous privacy program" since 2019. Despite these claims, the company's actions suggest a different approach to user privacy.
