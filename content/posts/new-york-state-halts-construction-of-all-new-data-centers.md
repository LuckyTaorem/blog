## Introduction to the Moratorium
New York State has taken a significant step by halting the construction of all new data centers. This decision was made by Governor Kathy Hochul, who signed an executive order temporarily barring the state from approving new permits for large data center projects. The move is aimed at addressing the environmental concerns associated with the construction and operation of data centers.

## Background and Motivation
The average data center built in the last few years has been smaller than 100 megawatts. However, through 2030, nearly a quarter of new data centers will exceed 500 megawatts, driven by increasing AI investment, according to [Bloomberg NEF](https://ltdeveloperblogs.github.io/posts/when-the-trump-administration-cracks-down-on-anthropic-who-benefits/). This growth has raised concerns about the impact of data centers on the environment, including their energy consumption and e-waste generation. ## Key Provisions of the Executive Order
The executive order applies to data centers 50 megawatts or larger. The state's Department of Environmental Conservation will not issue any permits that haven't already been completed. The moratorium will be lifted once the state finalizes an environmental review process for data centers, which Hochul expects will take about a year. Additionally, Hochul's office is considering requiring data centers to pay into a fund that would support the state's electrical grid.
