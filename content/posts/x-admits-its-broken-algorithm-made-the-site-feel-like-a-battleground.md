## Introduction to the Issue
X, a social media platform, has been facing issues with its algorithm, which was "missing" data about surfacing posts from people who you've followed back. This led to a "battleground" feel on the site, where users were more likely to engage with random arguments rather than posts from their mutuals. According to Nikita Bier, X's head of product, the algorithm's issue needed to be addressed to enhance the sense of community on the platform.

## The Algorithm's Issue
The algorithm's issue was causing posts from mutuals to be less visible, leading to a decrease in engagement and a sense of community on the site. This was not the intended behavior of the algorithm, and X's team has been working to fix the issue. As seen in other social media platforms, such as those discussed in [https://ltdeveloperblogs.github.io/posts/whatsapp-gets-new-chief-as-meta-taps-indias-cred-founder-kunal-shah-and-invests-900m-in-startup/](https://ltdeveloperblogs.github.io/posts/whatsapp-gets-new-chief-as-meta-taps-indias-cred-founder-kunal-shah-and-invests-900m-in-startup/), algorithms play a crucial role in shaping the user experience.

## The Tweak to Boost Visibility
To address the issue, X's team will be making a tweak to the algorithm to "boost visibility of your posts to your mutuals." This change aims to enhance the sense of community on the site by making posts from mutuals more visible. By doing so, X hopes to reduce the "battleground" feel on the site and create a more engaging and community-driven environment. This is similar to how other companies, like those mentioned in [https://ltdeveloperblogs.github.io/posts/valve-will-finally-let-you-build-your-own-steam-machine-with-steamos-for-desktop/](https://ltdeveloperblogs.github.io/posts/valve-will-finally-let-you-build-your-own-steam-machine-with-steamos-for-desktop/), prioritize community engagement and user experience.

## Impact on Users
The tweak to the algorithm is expected to have a positive impact on users, who will now see more posts from their mutuals. This will create a more engaging and community-driven environment, where users can interact with each other's posts and build stronger relationships.
