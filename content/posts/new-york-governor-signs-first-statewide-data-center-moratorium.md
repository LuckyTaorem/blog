## Introduction to Data Center Moratorium
New York Governor Kathy Hochul has signed an executive order enacting a one-year pause on the development of hyperscale data centers, marking the nation's first statewide data center moratorium. This move is aimed at addressing the environmental and energy impact of these massive facilities. As [reported earlier](https://ltdeveloperblogs.github.io/posts/new-york-becomes-the-first-state-to-enact-a-data-center-moratorium/), the moratorium is a significant step towards regulating the data center industry.

## Background and Context
The executive order pauses state environmental reviews for facilities over 50 megawatts for a year, instructing the state's Department of Public Service to assess the environmental and energy impact of data centers during this time. This move is part of a broader effort to regulate the data center industry, which has been growing rapidly in recent years. As discussed in our previous article on [Robotaxis Invade Closed Highways: A Tech Disaster?](https://ltdeveloperblogs.github.io/posts/waymo-recalls-nearly-4000-robotaxis-to-stop-them-driving-into-highway-construction-zones/), the tech industry is facing increasing scrutiny over its environmental impact.

### Key Players and Quotes
Several key players have weighed in on the moratorium, including Governor Hochul, who stated, "We have no choice but to address the challenges created by these massive facilities." Kristin Gonzalez, Democratic state senator and sponsor of the Data Center Development Act, added, "Technology should make our lives better, not pollute our water, strain our energy grid, or drive up our utility bills." Alex Beauchamp, Northeast regional director of Food and Water Watch, noted, "Any real moratorium is just a gigantic step forward."
