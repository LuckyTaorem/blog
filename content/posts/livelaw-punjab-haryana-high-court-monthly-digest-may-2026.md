## Introduction to the Punjab Haryana High Court
The Punjab Haryana High Court is a high court that has jurisdiction over the Indian states of Punjab and Haryana, as well as the Union Territory of Chandigarh. The court is located in Chandigarh, the capital city of both Punjab and Haryana. The Punjab Haryana High Court has a long history, dating back to 1919, when it was established as the Lahore High Court. After India gained independence in 1947, the court was renamed the Punjab High Court, and in 1966, it was renamed again to the Punjab and Haryana High Court, following the creation of the new state of Haryana.
