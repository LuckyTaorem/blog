## Background on Quantum Computing
Quantum computing is a type of computing that uses the principles of quantum mechanics to perform calculations. Unlike classical computers, which use bits to represent information, quantum computers use quantum bits or qubits, which can exist in multiple states simultaneously. This property allows quantum computers to process vast amounts of information in parallel, making them potentially much faster than classical computers for certain types of calculations.

The use of quantum computing in AI is still in its early stages, but it has the potential to revolutionize the field. ## The DTU Project
The DTU project, led by Professor Timothy Patrick Jenkins, used a quantum computer built by ORCA Computing, a British startup, to improve the accuracy of generative AI models used in drug discovery. The team used a hybrid technique that combined quantum and traditional processors to generate novel peptides, which are short chains of amino acids that can be used as drugs.

The project was funded by the Novo Nordisk Foundation, and the team included Jonathan Funk, a DTU Ph.D. student, and Richard Murray, the chief executive officer of ORCA Computing. The use of quantum computing in this project allowed the team to explore a much larger space of possible drug candidates than would have been possible with classical computers.
