## Introduction to PixVerse
PixVerse, a video-generation startup, has recently raised $439 million in a Series C extension, bringing its total valuation to over $2 billion. This significant funding round is a testament to the company's innovative approach to video generation and its potential to disrupt the industry. As [reported by TechCrunch journalists like Ivan Mehta](https://ltdeveloperblogs.github.io/posts/ubers-product-chief-on-hotels-robotaxis-and-why-the-company-doesnt-want-to-be-everything-for-everyone/), the startup's co-founders, Wang Changhu and Jaden Xie, have a strong background in AI and visual understanding technology.

## Key Investors and Funding
The Series C extension was led by prominent investors, including CDH Investments, Alibaba, Lollapalooza Capital, Ivy Capital, Grand Mount Capital, Eastern Bell Capital, Mirae Asset, Blue Focus, Cloud Alpha, i Globe Partners, and OCBC's Lion X Ventures. The funding round demonstrates the confidence of investors in PixVerse's technology and its potential for growth. ## Products and Features
PixVerse offers a range of video generation models, including the V-Series for consumer and API use, the C-Series for professional film and commercial workflows, and the R-Series of world models for game development and world building. The company is also planning to launch a new V-Series model for video generation and a new version of its world model later this year. These products and features demonstrate PixVerse's commitment to providing high-quality video generation solutions for various industries.
