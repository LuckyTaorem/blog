## Introduction to the Incident
The Iranian government has been reported to have abused vulnerabilities in the global telecoms infrastructure to locate U.S. military personnel in the build-up to the Iran War and in the early days of the conflict. This incident highlights the risks associated with the exploitation of telecoms infrastructure vulnerabilities, which can have severe consequences for national security.

## The Exploited Vulnerabilities
The Iranian government exploited the Signaling System 7 (SS7) vulnerability, a well-known issue in the global telecoms infrastructure. SS7 is a set of protocols used in 2G and 3G networks, and its exploitation can allow attackers to track cellphones abroad. In addition to SS7, the Iranian government also abused advertising technology used to serve tailored ads to cellphone users. This abuse of advertising technology allowed the Iranian government to gather information about U.S. military personnel, including their locations.

## The Impact of the Exploitation
The exploitation of these vulnerabilities had significant consequences, including the location of U.S. military forces stationed in military bases and hotels in Iraq, Bahrain, and other countries in the Middle East.
