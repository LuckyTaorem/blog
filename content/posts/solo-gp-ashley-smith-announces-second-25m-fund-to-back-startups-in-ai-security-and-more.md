## Introduction to Vermilion Cliffs Ventures
Vermilion Cliffs Ventures, a venture capital firm founded by solo GP Ashley Smith, has announced the close of its second fund, Fund II, with a total of $25 million in capital commitments. This new fund will focus on investing in early-stage startups in the AI, security, and infrastructure spaces. As a seasoned marketing expert with experience working at companies like Twilio, Facebook, GitHub, and GitLab, Ashley Smith brings a unique perspective to the venture capital industry.

## Background on Ashley Smith
Ashley Smith's experience in marketing has given her a deep understanding of the challenges that founders face when trying to sell to developers and security teams. In her own words, "Selling to developers and security teams is its own discipline, and most founders learn it the expensive way with time." She aims to use her expertise to help founders avoid common mistakes and achieve success more quickly. As she notes, "I try to help them skip mistakes I've made or seen other founders make." This approach has already borne fruit in her first fund, which invested in 35 companies, including Keycard, a cybersecurity startup, and Copilot Kit, an AI infrastructure company.

## Investment Strategy
The investment strategy for Fund II will be similar to that of Fund I, with a focus on early-stage startups in the AI, security, and infrastructure spaces. The average check size for investments will be between $500,000 and $1 million, and the fund plans to invest in at least 25 companies over the next 2.5 years. This strategy is designed to provide founders with the resources they need to grow and scale their businesses, while also giving Vermilion Cliffs Ventures the opportunity to work closely with a diverse range of startups.

### Key Areas of Focus
Some of the key areas that Vermilion Cliffs Ventures will be focusing on with Fund II include:
* AI and machine learning: This includes startups working on AI-powered solutions for industries like healthcare, finance, and education.
* Security: This includes startups working on cybersecurity solutions, such as threat detection, incident response, and security orchestration.
* Infrastructure: This includes startups working on cloud infrastructure, data storage, and networking solutions.
