## Introduction to Apple's M7 and M8 Processors
Apple's latest advancements in processor technology, the M7 and M8, have been making waves in the tech industry. According to [Apple's M6 Era Shortlived](https://ltdeveloperblogs.github.io/posts/m6-era-will-last-just-six-months-as-apple-pushes-for-ai-focused-m7/), the company is shifting its focus towards AI-centric processors. The M7 and M8 chips are expected to be used in the 16-inch MacBook Pro and Apple Intelligence servers, respectively.

## The Role of Apple Car Research
The development of the M7 and M8 processors has been influenced by Apple's cancelled Apple Car project. After investing $10 billion over 10 years, Apple decided to repurpose the research to improve artificial intelligence performance in its processors. As [Apple Skips M6 Pro Chips](https://ltdeveloperblogs.github.io/posts/heres-why-apple-is-reportedly-skipping-m6-pro-and-m6-max-chips/) suggests, the company is prioritizing AI support over overall speed and power efficiency.

## Key Features and Products
The M7 and M8 processors boast several notable features, including:
* Improved artificial intelligence performance
* Enhanced processing power for the 16-inch MacBook Pro and Apple Intelligence servers
* Concentration on AI support over overall speed and power efficiency, as reported by [Bloomberg](https://ltdeveloperblogs.github.io/posts/m6-era-will-last-just-six-months-as-apple-pushes-for-ai-focused-m7/)
The M7 Pro processor is specifically designed for the 16-inch MacBook Pro, while the M8 processor is tailored for Apple Intelligence servers.

## Pricing and Release Dates
Unfortunately, no specific pricing information or release dates have been announced for the M7 and M8 processors.
